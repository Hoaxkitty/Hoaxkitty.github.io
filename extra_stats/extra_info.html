Religion, Piety & Reformation Beliefs:

Piety religious discount only applies to buildings, prophets/missionaries and military units.

Jesuit Education can be used by anyone who's city is religious

To the Glory of God (great people) is only for the religion founder

Initiation rights is 66g/city, 33f per great person expended, but the wonder is 100g

Great People:

Scientists (+8), engineers (+4), and merchants (+4) all share same counter

Great prophet overlaps with them only if from a wonder/policy; everyone else is independent

Piety Prophet is free; Babylon Great Scientist is Free; Optics GM is free

Liberty, Hagia Sophia, Leaning Tower, Taj Mahal, Porcelain, Hubble, Mayans, Collective rule, Great Merchant, Aesthetic 3, Globe, and Order Tier 3 aren't

Purchasing great people (except for GP) doesn't increase counter

Purchasing great artist types with faith/social policy and futurism adds tourism; no modifiers

Counter starts at 67 and moves up by 67 (67, 134, 201, 268, 335, etc.)

All specialists provide 3gpp per turn

-25% prophet cost from enhancer counts toward purchasing them too

Culture from planted great prophets doesn't add tourism with hotels/airports

Great people die to nukes, even in cities with a bomb shelter

There is a 5% base chance to spawn a prophet per turn, additional points of faith add 1% each

Ideology Wonders:

Prora is coastal only, not lake; Kremlin bonus is local city only

Spies:

Spies steal 25% faster per rank

Most expensive tech available at insertion*1.25) / ((science*.5)*(1+.25x)) x=spy promotions

Counterspy is 33/36/40 percent chance to kill

Autocracy tenet is 2x, so 2/2.5/3.0 for promoted spies

Captured Cities & Puppeted Cities:

When your capital is lost, the next largest city becomes capital; unsure what breaks ties

1 turn of resistance per surviving population

Puppets are -25% all yields, same unhappy as regular; increase science (not culture) +5%

Purchase-cost modifiers:

Big Ben (.15) and Mercantilism (.25; com 3) are additive so everything costs .60 of base cost

Mobilization (.33) and Skyscrapers (.33) are multiplicative, so .67 of base cost

All three combined are (1*.6*.66) =.396 or .40 of base cost

Landsknecht 160 (pikes 270) base cost, 195g to upgrade, keep the no-pillage and double plunder

Air Combat:

Land based SAM defend up to 2 squares away, once per turn

Air based is up to fighter range, 1 fighter per bomber, each fighter may only intercept once

Destroyers have a 40% interception chance, inside 2 tiles

Missile cruisers have 100% chance to intercept

Rebase is 2x the tile range of the unit

Roads & Forts:

Neutral roads are paid by whoever built them

Roads in razed borders and in city-states are free; Forts are free

Railroads are 5 tiles

City-States:

Base Decay-Normal: 1.25, Hostile: 1.87

Patronage or Religion-Normal: 0.83, Hostile: 1.4; Both-Normal: 0.62, Hostile: 0.93

Military grant 13/11; Maritime grant 3/1 and 2/0

Religious-Anc/Clas: 2/4, Med/Ren: 4/8, Ind and Beyond: 8/16

Cultural-Anc/Clas: 3/6, Med/Ren: 6/12, Ind and Beyond: 13/26

Nukes:

Nukes do 25 damage to units in a bomb sheltered base, so 4 to kill full hp units

50% of remaining city HP

Kills units on direct hits including bomber stacks

Indirect hits still kill population and cities

Nuke Missiles destroy non-capital cities without shelters at less than 6 population

Nuke Missiles destroy non-capital cities with shelters at less than 5 population

Nukes kill other nukes and great people.

Bulbing:

Great Scientist/Writer: (Avg science for last 8 turns)*5.35

Great Engineer: 201+20.1*(pop)

Misc:

Crop yield is worked food and caravan food. Happiness is 60 +/- (3*Current Happiness)

Finishing aqueducts on same turn as city growth doesn't give any bonus (linear)

Fertilizer is +1f from pastures, plantations and inland farms

Golden age culture boost includes city-states and aesthetics happiness culture
